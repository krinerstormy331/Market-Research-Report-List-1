<p><p>Electrostatic chucks (ESCs) are essential in ion implantation equipment, providing stable wafer retention during processing. The Coulomb type utilizes electric fields to create attractive forces, ensuring precise wafer placement. In contrast, the Johnsen-Rahbek (JR) type employs a combination of electrostatic attraction and mechanical action, offering enhanced grip stability even under varying conditions. Both types serve critical roles in semiconductor manufacturing, improving efficiency and accuracy while minimizing wafer damage during ion implantation processes.</p></p>
