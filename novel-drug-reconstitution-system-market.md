<p><p>The Novel Drug Reconstitution System market features two primary types: bottled and bagged systems. Bottled systems involve pre-filled vials where the drug is stored as a powder and requires reconstitution with a solvent before administration. This format ensures stability and ease of transport. In contrast, bagged systems utilize flexible bags that contain the drug in powder form, combined with saline or another diluent, allowing for a more compact and user-friendly option for healthcare providers. Both types enhance drug delivery efficiency.</p></p>
