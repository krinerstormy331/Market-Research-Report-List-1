<p><p>An Implantable Collamer Lens (ICL) is a type of lens designed to be surgically implanted in the eye to correct refractive vision errors, such as myopia, hyperopia, and astigmatism. Unlike traditional contact lenses, ICLs remain in the eye permanently and are made from a biocompatible material known as Collamer, which combines collagen and polymer. This innovative procedure offers an alternative to laser vision correction and is particularly suitable for patients with high degrees of refractive error or thin corneas.</p><p>The Implantable Collamer Lens Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 13.6% during the forecast period. Several factors contribute to this growth, including an increasing prevalence of vision impairments globally and rising awareness about advanced surgical options for vision correction. Additionally, technological advancements in lens design and implantation techniques are driving demand. Emerging markets are witnessing a rise in medical tourism for vision correction, further bolstering market growth. </p><p>Trends such as personalized ICL options, enhanced safety profiles, and improved patient outcomes are also shaping the market landscape, as healthcare providers focus on patient-specific solutions and better surgical techniques to minimize complications and promote faster recovery.</p></p>
