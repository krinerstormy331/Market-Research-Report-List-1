<p><p>Chemistry models facilitate the visualization of molecular structures and interactions. The Ball and Spoke model represents atoms as spheres (balls) connected by rods (spokes) to indicate bond lengths and angles. Space-Filling models depict atoms as spheres that overlap to show the actual space a molecule occupies, highlighting relative sizes. Other models include wireframe representations for clarity in understanding bond arrangements and electron configurations. Each model type serves different purposes in education, research, and molecular design, enhancing comprehension of chemical compounds.</p></p>
