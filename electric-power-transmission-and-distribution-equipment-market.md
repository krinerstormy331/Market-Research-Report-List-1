<p><p>Electric power transmission and distribution equipment encompasses systems that transport electricity from generation sources to consumers. Overhead transmission involves the use of elevated power lines supported by towers or poles, offering cost-effective installation and maintenance, though it is susceptible to weather-related disruptions. Conversely, underground transmission employs buried cables, providing enhanced reliability and reduced aesthetic impact but at higher installation costs. Both methods play vital roles in delivering electricity efficiently while catering to different environmental and urban needs.</p></p>
