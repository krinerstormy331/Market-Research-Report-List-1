<p><p>The wire rods market comprises two primary types: electrical and mechanical. Electrical wire rods are designed for conductivity, commonly used in power transmission, telecommunications, and electrical applications. These rods often emphasize low resistivity and thermal stability. Conversely, mechanical wire rods are engineered for strength and durability, suitable for construction, automotive, and industrial applications. They typically feature higher tensile strength and flexibility, making them ideal for producing fasteners, springs, and other structural components. Both types play crucial roles in their respective industries.</p></p>
