<p><p>The thoracic endoprosthesis market is primarily divided into two types: aorta endoprosthesis and branch endoprosthesis. Aorta endoprosthesis is designed to treat aneurysms and dissections in the aortic arch or descending aorta, providing stability and reducing the risk of rupture. Branch endoprosthesis, on the other hand, is utilized for addressing issues in the branching vessels off the main aorta, ensuring blood flow to vital organs. Both types enhance patient outcomes and are crucial in thoracic aortic interventions.</p></p>
