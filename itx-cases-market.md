<p><p>ITX cases come in two primary types: vertical and horizontal. Vertical cases are designed to stand upright, enabling efficient use of space and better airflow, making them ideal for compact builds. They allow for easy accessibility and component organization in a small footprint. Conversely, horizontal cases lie flat, resembling traditional desktop designs, which can facilitate stacked configurations and offer more stability for larger hardware. Both styles cater to diverse user needs and preferences in compact computing environments.</p></p>
